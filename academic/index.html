BAO Reconstruction with Photometric Redshift Smearing
Arizona Cosmology Lab, University of Arizona
Research Assistant · Mentor: Prof. Tim Eifler
Jan 2026 — PresentTucson, AZ
Building a BAO analysis pipeline that quantifies how photometric redshift smearing biases standard-ruler calibration — a key systematic for next-generation surveys — with a forward model that propagates photo-z uncertainty through reconstruction. First-author paper in preparation.
Standard rulersForward modelingRoman · Rubin
Methods & current results
  • Systematics: cross-validated two covariance estimators across a 250-cell sensitivity grid, reducing an MCMC inference bias by 4–6×.
  • Monte Carlo: mapped a 25-realization ensemble to locate where reconstruction's 20–40% precision benefit becomes statistically insignificant under increasing photo-z smearing.
  • Ownership: lead the analysis from simulated-catalog generation through Bayesian posterior inference with a PI and postdoctoral collaborator.
Predicting Power-Spectrum Window Functions with Neural Networks
SPHEREx Team, Caltech (NASA / JPL)
Summer Research Fellow (SURF) · Mentor: Dr. Olivier Doré
Jun 2024 — Aug 2024Pasadena, CA
Built a ResUNet convolutional network in PyTorch to deconvolve instrument window-function effects from galaxy power spectra to sub-percent error, improving constraints on local primordial non-Gaussianity fNL. Owned the full pipeline: lognormal mock catalogs with real SPHEREx masks, distributed HPC training, and validation against analytic baselines.
PyTorch · ResUNetPrimordial non-GaussianityInflation
Research deliverables
  • Generated masked lognormal mock catalogs, ran distributed Linux-HPC training, and benchmarked the learned deconvolution against analytic baselines.
  • Delivered the proposal, two interim reports, final presentation, and final report to the SPHEREx faculty committee.
